A Passage to India

"A Passage to India," a novel by E.M. Forster, explores the complexities of friendship and prejudice in British-ruled India during the 1920s. It follows the story of Dr. Aziz, a Muslim doctor, and his interactions with two English visitors, Mrs. Moore and Miss Adela Quested, as they navigate the cultural and social landscape of India. A seemingly innocent incident in the Marabar Caves sparks a series of events that unravel friendships and expose deep-seated racial tensions between the British and the Indian community. The novel delves into themes of colonialism, racism, and the search for understanding between different cultures. Through its characters, Forster explores the challenges of bridging cultural divides and the potential for both friendship and conflict in a world marked by power imbalances. The story highlights the complexities of human relationships and the enduring impact of prejudice on individuals and societies. .

Published Date: 6 April 1924

Director: E.M. Forster
