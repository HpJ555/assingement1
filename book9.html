The Invisible Man

novel by Ralph Ellison that follows a nameless Black protagonist as he navigates a racially charged America. The narrator feels invisible because people fail to see him, and he journeys from the South to Harlem, grappling with identity, racism, and societal expectations. The novel explores the psychological and social effects of racism, offering a powerful critique of systemic discrimination and the dehumanization of Black individuals, according to SparkNotes.
